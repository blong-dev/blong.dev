Gnosis deployed
The private, event-driven AI platform behind everything else here.
case study

What it does Public and private data flows through an ingestion layer into a knowledge graph and a time-series warehouse, where an autonomous agent layer reasons over it and ships results to a live dashboard.

The infrastructure flex Go services move work across a message bus where every agent action is an auditable event — no agent calls another directly. Postgres, Neo4j, Elasticsearch, and Redis sit behind it, held together by ~2,200 automated tests. It runs on two consumer desktops over a 10 GbE link — most of the intelligence is local, so there's no per-token bill for the volume work.

How it's operated I set direction; the platform runs the loop — roughly two dozen agents and services keep it moving without me in the path.

The workforce

A named team on one custom, bus-native harness — every action a logged, replayable event. Three run persistently; the rest spawn on demand. Backend (local Qwen or frontier Claude) is a config line, switchable per agent.

case study

dreamtree in flight
A public ledger where every person, agent, and thing owns proof of what it creates.
case study

What it does A public ledger where every person, organization, agent, or thing holds the permanent, verifiable record of what it creates. Your data is already yours; dreamtree is how you prove it — with no company in the middle who can revoke it.

The infrastructure flex An L1 on Cosmos SDK + CometBFT with a novel attestation-as-work consensus: the network settles records by attesting to them, weighted by a reputation signal with asymmetric decay so bad actors fade and good ones compound. Records carry W3C signatures (eddsa-jcs-2022) and did:webvh identity, so they verify anywhere, not only inside dreamtree.

The build Designed and built the whole stack solo — the chain modules (seeds, photons, attestation, reputation, licensing), the off-chain anchoring service, and roots, the wallet at the human edge. Running on private infrastructure.

roots in flight
Hold your identity and credentials, and prove them to anyone — no company in the middle.
case study

What it does The wallet at the human edge of dreamtree: where a person or organization holds their identity and the verifiable credentials others issue them, and can prove any of it to anyone — without the issuer in the loop.

The infrastructure flex A Cloudflare Workers + D1 service that issues W3C Verifiable Credentials with real Data Integrity proofs (eddsa-jcs-2022), serves did:web / did:webvh documents, and gates every read behind consent with a full audit trail. Every record anchors to the dreamtree chain for tamper-evidence.

The agent angle An MCP endpoint lets agents verify credentials too, and it implements the CAWG identity-claims-aggregation profile — the credentials that ride inside C2PA content provenance.

turbo3 open-source fork
My CUDA port of the published TurboQuant method — 524K context on one 32 GB GPU.
case study

What it does A CUDA implementation of TurboQuant (ICLR 2026) KV-cache compression for llama.cpp — squeezing the KV cache down to 3.5 bits so one GPU holds far more context.

The infrastructure flex Configured and validated on Qwen3.5-27B to fit 524K tokens (3 slots × 174K) at ~55 tok/s on a single RTX 5090 — ~4.6× smaller KV than FP16. It works because the model is hybrid: only 16 of 64 layers carry a KV cache worth compressing.

What's mine vs. what isn't The TurboQuant algorithm and its 3.5-bit math are published research (ICLR 2026, arXiv:2504.19874) — not mine. The CUDA port to llama.cpp, the VRAM tuning, and the validation on Qwen3.5-27B are — which is what gives the agents the long context to digest filings and papers in one pass.
